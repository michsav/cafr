Significant Accounting Policies Used in Preparing the SEFA

 

The accompanying Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (Schedule) includes the federal grant activity of Kyrene Elementary School District No. 28 under programs of the federal government for the year ended June 30, 2019. The information in the Schedule is presented in accordance with the requirements of the Uniform Guidance. Because the Schedule presents only a selected portion of the operations of the District, it is not intended to and does not present the financial position, changes in net position or cash flows of the District.Expenditures reported on the Schedule are reported on the modified accrual basis of accounting. Such expenditures are recognized following the cost principles contained in the applicable Uniform Guidance, wherein certain types of expenditures are not allowable or are limited as to reimbursement. Any negative amounts shown on the Schedule represent adjustments or credits made in the normal course of business to amounts reported as expenditures in prior years. Pass‐through entity identifying numbers are presented where available.

 

10% De Minimis Cost Rate

 

The auditee did not use the de minimis cost rate.

 

Catalogue of Federal Domestic Assistance Numbers

 

The program titles and CFDA numbers were obtained from the federal or pass‐through grantor or the 2019 Catalog of Federal Domestic Assistance. When no CFDA number had been assigned to a program, the two‐ digit federal agency identifier, a period, and the federal contract number were used. When there was no federal contract number, the two‐digit federal agency identifier, a period, and the word unknown were used.

FINDINGS RELATED TO FINANCIAL STATEMENTS REPORTED IN ACCORDANCE WITH GOVERNMENT AUDITING STANDARDS

 

Finding Number: FS-2019-001

 

Repeat Finding: No

Type of Finding: Significant Deficiency

Description: Internal Controls Over Capital Assets

 

 

CRITERIA

 

District management is responsible for establishing and maintaining internal controls that include controls for the generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) basis financial statements issued by the District. The District’s system of internal controls must extend beyond the cash basis general ledger and the subsidiary schedules prepared by the District; rather, it must also include controls over the GAAP basis financial statements. Both the Uniform System of Financial Records (USFR) guidelines and an appropriate internal control system require the District’s records of capital assets to agree to supporting documentation and the prior fiscal year schedule.

 

CONDITION

 

Inadequate internal controls existed over capital assets. The capital asset schedule was not prepared in accordance with District policies, the current fiscal year beginning balances did not agree to the prior fiscal year ending balances, and the capital asset schedule’s inputs did not agree to supporting documentation. In addition, the asset listing within the District capital asset software system contained numerous errors that were not resolved timely. Lastly, capital expenditures were not properly reconciled to capital additions, resulting in audit adjustments.

 

CAUSE

 

There is a lack of effective internal controls over the capital asset schedule.

 

EFFECT

 

Without a properly completed capital asset schedule, the District may not be able to effectively manage its assets and resources. Errors in the capitalization and recording of assets may not be caught and corrected in a timely manner. Audit adjustments were necessary to properly report the balances and activity of the District’s financial statements.

 

CONTEXT

 

The District lacked adequate internal controls over capital assets. During our review of the capital asset listings, we noted the following deficiencies:

 

The capital asset listing contained numerous negative asset balances that were being incorrectly depreciated. Due to the significance of the misstatement, beginning balances were required to be restated for $570,192.

The initial capital asset listing’s beginning balances did not agree to the prior fiscal year’s ending balances, which resulted in audit adjustments.


 

 

 


KYRENE ELEMENTARY SCHOOL DISTRICT NO. 28 SCHEDULE OF FINDINGS AND QUESTIONED COSTS YEAR ENDED JUNE 30, 2019

 

 

 

FINDINGS RELATED TO FINANCIAL STATEMENTS REPORTED IN ACCORDANCE WITH GOVERNMENT AUDITING STANDARDS

 

Finding Number: FS-2019-001

 

CONTEXT

 

Errors were noted in the additions and depreciation calculations.

 

There were numerous differences in vehicles, furniture, and equipment additions and deletions that caused the ending balance not to agree to supporting documentation by $149,592.

 

The District paid $302,122 for construction services relating to the subsequent fiscal year out of current fiscal year monies.

 

RECOMMENDATION

 

The District should design and implement effective internal control processes and procedures to ensure the financial statements and related notes are free from material misstatements. Specifically, the District should ensure that a complete and accurate capital asset schedule is completed in a timely manner. This review should include a reconciliation of prior year listings and a careful review of construction in progress at year end.
